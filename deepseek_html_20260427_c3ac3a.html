The Foundation

The Five Pillars
of Islam

The five pillars are the essential acts of worship that form the core of Muslim life. They are the framework through which a believer draws closer to Allah.

الشَّهَادَة
Shahada
The declaration of faith: "There is no god but Allah, and Muhammad is His messenger."
الصَّلَاة
Salah
Five daily prayers performed at dawn, midday, afternoon, sunset, and night — orienting the heart toward Mecca.
الزَّكَاة
Zakat
Mandatory almsgiving — 2.5% of savings given to the needy each year, purifying wealth and nurturing community.
الصَّوْم
Sawm
Fasting during Ramadan — abstaining from food, drink, and ill conduct from dawn to sunset for an entire month.
الْحَجّ
Hajj
The pilgrimage to Mecca — an obligation once in a lifetime for those able, uniting millions in profound humility before God.

Aqeedah

The Six Articles
of Faith

Belief in Allah

Absolute monotheism — Tawhid — the conviction that Allah alone is God, unique, without partner, equal, or offspring. He is Al-Ahad: The One.

Belief in the Angels

Angels are light-formed beings who carry out Allah's commands — Jibril, Mikail, Israfil — unseen servants of divine order.

Belief in the Holy Books

The Quran, the Torah, the Psalms, and the Gospel — divine revelations sent to guide humanity across the ages. The Quran is the final, preserved word.

Belief in the Prophets

From Adam to Muhammad ﷺ — 124,000 messengers chosen to deliver God's guidance. Muhammad ﷺ is the Seal of Prophethood.

Belief in the Day of Judgement

Every soul shall be resurrected and held accountable before Allah — a day when justice is absolute and mercy is vast.

Belief in Divine Decree

Qadr — the certainty that Allah's knowledge encompasses all things, and that both good and hardship are within His wise plan.

Al-Tarikh

A Civilization
of Light & Knowledge

610 CE
The First Revelation

In the Cave of Hira, Angel Jibril descended upon Muhammad ﷺ with the first verses of the Quran: "Read in the name of your Lord who created." A new dawn for humanity had arrived.

622 CE
The Hijra — The Migration

The Prophet ﷺ and his companions migrated from Mecca to Medina, establishing the first Muslim community and marking Year 1 of the Islamic calendar.

632 CE
The Farewell Sermon

From the plains of Arafat, the Prophet ﷺ delivered his final sermon: "All mankind is from Adam and Eve. An Arab has no superiority over a non-Arab."

8th–13th C
The Golden Age of Islam

Muslim scholars pioneered algebra, astronomy, medicine, and philosophy. Baghdad's House of Wisdom preserved knowledge — lighting Europe's path out of the Dark Ages.

Today
1.9 Billion Voices

Islam is the world's second-largest religion, practiced across every continent — a living, breathing community bound not by race, but by faith, prayer, and love for the Divine.
